2. Purpose & Privacy by Design

AI Artist Skipper lets you skip tracks by a curated list of presumed AI-generated artists on Spotify. By design, we do not process or store any personal user data on our servers. All Spotify-related interactions take place entirely in your browser (Privacy by Design, Art. 25 GDPR).

3. System Architecture

  • Frontend (SPA): Runs completely in your browser.
  • Backend (Node.js/Express): Serves static files and a minimal JSON API, hosted by Uberspace GmbH (Mainz, Germany).
  • Database (MySQL): Also hosted by Uberspace GmbH in Germany.

5. Data Flows Overview

A) Frontend (Browser)

  • OAuth PKCE Flow: You enter your Spotify Client ID; a code_verifier is generated locally. The OAuth exchange happens directly between your browser and Spotify; we never see or store your tokens.
  • Playback Calls: Made straight from your browser to the Spotify Web API.
  • API Calls: GET /api/artists (no personal data) and POST /api/submit (artist name + Spotify ID).

B) Backend (Uberspace, Germany)

  • Serves static assets and the two API endpoints.
  • No application-level logging of IPs or tokens.
  • Host logs: IP, timestamp and User-Agent are pseudonymised immediately and deleted after ≤ 7 days to safeguard security and availability (legitimate interest).

C) Database (Uberspace, Germany)

Stores only non-personal data: artists, submissions, daily_song.

7. Cookies, Local Storage & Consent Management

We use ccm19 to collect and store your cookie preferences. You can revisit them at any time via the “Cookie Settings” link in the footer (Art. 7 (3) GDPR - withdrawal must be as easy as consent).

  • Necessary cookies/local storage objects are set under § 25 (2) No. 2 TTDSG because they are technically required to provide the service (e.g. OAuth tokens to maintain the current playback session).
  • Functionality cookies (Spotify Web Playback SDK) load only after you grant “Functionality” consent. When you donate via PayPal, you leave our site; PayPal's own cookies and privacy notices apply there.

Details and lifetimes are available in Annex A at the end of this document.

8. International Data Transfers

Spotify may process personal data (e.g. IP address, tokens) on servers outside the EEA. According to Spotify's Privacy Policy, transfers rely on:

  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved by the European Commission,
  • Adequacy decisions (Japan, Canada, South Korea, UK, Switzerland) and
  • Supplementary measures such as encryption and access controls.

B) Gmail Support Communication

Google LLC processes support e-mails on global infrastructure (incl. the USA). Transfers are covered by:

  • The EU-US Data Privacy Framework (DPF), and
  • The SCCs for any other locations.

C) PayPal Donations

When you follow our paypal.me link to donate, your transaction is processed by PayPal (Europe) S.à r.l. et Cie or its affiliated entities. PayPal may transfer data outside the EEA under the EU-US DPF and SCCs. Please refer to PayPal's privacy statement for details. We do not receive your payment details.
